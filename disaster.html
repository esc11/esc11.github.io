Disaster Daily

  2 weeks
  "Mash-up" two APIs with Python

Using Twitter and the humanitarian information portal ReliefWeb, I developed a website that displays information and relevant tweets on the eight latest natural disasters in the world. As a buffer from the large amounts of text, I also used Flickr's API to display an image with its respective country as its keyword.
Many of these crises do not get much news coverage, so quite a few do not have any tweets. If a country has more than two natural disasters, such as two cyclones, they will appear twice on the site.

This class project was designed to display mastery of Python and use of APIs, and HTML/CSS wasn't expected to be understood too thoroughly. Thus, I took it as a challenge to make this site look aesthetically pleasant. More than anything else, I wanted to have the disaster content and the tweets to be read in similar areas of the screen. My rationale was that if read together and associated together, the cold facts of the disaster could instead be humanized and present another perspective of the news to the user.
