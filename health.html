Surprisingly enough, a hospital review site didn't exist, so we built a website to search for hospitals in Washington by zip code, city, or hospital name, and once a user creates an account, they can post reviews about their visits. Reviews can still be viewed without an account. Data was retrieved from Medicare.gov.

While this project was more focused on development, we came across ethical issues when attemping to determine what aspects of a hospital a patient should judge. We could highlight how child-friendly, clean, inexpensive, efficient a hospital could be, but with an immense amount of data, it wasn't easy to narrow down what was needed and what may have been unnecessary. We were essentially deciding what a user should value in a hospital, and that was highly unsettling. Given the time constraint and the inability to extensively research, this project was disappointing to me - we couldn't identify clear personas and their values. That being said, it introduced me to what I need to study and define in future projects, and it motivated me to care more about the user.

I was responsible for designing the interface, finding and parsing the data, and creating page for individual hospitals with the Google Maps API.
